Identify before you buy

Choose by the failed seat in front of you.

Start with the chair problem: a groove, drilled holes, four rails, a mid-century frame, or a rocker that needs woven tape. Each path points to materials, sizing goods, and starter tools that belong together.

01

Grooved frame with old cane sheet

Look for a routed channel around the seat. You likely need cane webbing, reed spline, wedges, glue, and a trimming chisel.

Pressed-cane dining chair kit
02

Rows of drilled holes around the rail

Count holes and check spacing. Hand-caning strand, binder cane, pegs, awl, and a pattern card will keep the weave ordered.

Hand-caning supplies
03

Ladder-back or trapezoid seat rails

Paper rush, natural rush, seagrass, and corner-forming guides support tight, even seats without guessing bundle length.

04

Open frame for cord or tape

Danish cord benches and Shaker tape rockers need exact width, rail layout, fasteners, and a clear crossing sequence.

Sizing and prep

The small measuring goods that prevent the wrong order.

Before glue, soaking, or first pass, use a measuring card, spline sample, and practice frame to confirm the fit. Rush & Reed treats prep supplies as part of the repair, not an afterthought.

Groove width Match spline after removing old cane.
Hole spacing Select cane strand by center-to-center distance.
Rail length Estimate rush, cord, or tape yardage before starting.
