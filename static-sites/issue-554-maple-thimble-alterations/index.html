Before you come in

Bring the pieces that make the fit honest.

Good tailoring starts with context. The studio will help you decide what is worth altering, what can be repaired cleanly, and when a practical workaround is better than overworking the garment.

For a smoother fitting

  • Wear or bring the shoes and underlayers you plan to use.
  • Bring every piece in a suit or set, even if only one item needs work.
  • Share your deadline before pins go in.
  • Point out previous repairs, stains, or fragile fabric areas.

The shop story

A repair-first counter, a steady fitting room, and advice you can use.

Maple & Thimble is shaped around the kind of sewing work neighbors need every week: saving a torn jacket lining, making thrifted trousers fit, getting a wedding-party hem finished carefully, or deciding whether a repair is worth the cost. The shop keeps the process direct, warm, and clear.
