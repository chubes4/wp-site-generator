First visit

A predictable intake process keeps the handoff low-stress.

Before a dog joins regular daycare, the team gathers the practical details that make supervised play safer and more comfortable.

  1. 1

    Send the basics

    Tell us your dog’s age, size, play history, training notes, health needs, and whether they are anxious, shy, senior, or high-energy.

  2. 2

    Share records and routines

    Bring vaccination records, feeding instructions, medication notes, collar and leash details, and emergency contact information.

  3. 3

    Try a short yard introduction

    Staff introduce your dog slowly, watch body language, test rest breaks, and decide which group rhythm feels appropriate.

  4. 4

    Leave with a clear next step

    You receive honest fit notes, schedule recommendations, grooming add-on options, and any concerns to revisit before a longer day.

Supervised playgroups

We separate the yard by confidence, not chaos.

Dogs rotate through indoor play, fenced yard time, enrichment stations, and nap rooms. Staff stay at dog level, redirect before play escalates, and keep notes on energy, appetite, bathroom breaks, and interactions.

Good fit questions are welcome.

Ask about leash reactivity, senior mobility, tiny dogs, rescue decompression, adolescent energy, or dogs who need a quieter introduction. If we are not the right setting, we will say so clearly.
