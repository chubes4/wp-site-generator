Sizing rule

Measure the hole, not the broken rung.

Old chair parts shrink, oval, and wear unevenly. For repair stock, measure the mortise in two directions and choose the nearest diameter below the smallest reading.

  1. Mark inside diameter at the chair post.
  2. Confirm shoulder-to-shoulder length.
  3. Select dry oak for rungs, maple for legs, walnut for wedges.

Repair use cases

Keep good chairs in service with parts that actually match.

Loose kitchen chair rung

Choose a white-oak replacement by diameter, cut to shoulder length, and wedge or glue according to the original joint.

5/8" and 3/4" stock

Torn cane or rush seat

Order cane webbing or paper rush with a tool-light weaving guide, sized to small frames before you buy a full coil.

woven-seat materials

Missing wedge or peg

Use walnut wedge packs and straight-grain pegs matched for visible chair joints, not generic hardware-store dowel.
