First visit essentials

What it feels like to walk in

Our storefront has a glass door, a small lobby board, and a black-box room just beyond the curtain. You do not need theater vocabulary to belong here.

Finding the entrance

Look for the painted crown above the brick alley window. Staff or volunteers greet guests at the lobby table.

Seating

Flexible rows hold 48 people. Tell us if you need an aisle seat, wheelchair space, or a seat near the exit.

Family guidance

Show pages note recommended ages. Youth showcases and preview evenings are relaxed and family-friendly.

Low-pressure room

Workshops and open nights include clear introductions, opt-out choices, and no surprise audience participation.

Space use

A flexible room for local work

The black-box is available for rehearsal blocks, small rentals, artist residencies, school partnerships, and neighborhood organization meetings when the season calendar allows.

Rehearsal blocks

Evening and weekend slots for small casts, table reads, and movement rehearsals.

Community meetings

Chairs, folding tables, lobby check-in, and a quiet room for group conversation.

School partnerships

Matinee visits, teen ensemble showcases, and guided introductions to backstage roles.

Artist residencies

Short room-use periods for local makers developing readings, salons, or small performance experiments.

Seen around the room

Storefront light, marked scripts, and work in progress
