First-visit guidance

What happens when you walk in

We keep the studio calm, clear, and usable. A staff member greets you, shows where to store belongings, explains cleanup, and helps you understand which wheel, table, shelf, or glaze area is yours for the session.

Bring Closed-toe shoes, short nails if possible, and clothing that can get muddy.
Provided Aprons, bats, starter tools, towels, basic underglazes, and studio-safe instruction.
Access Step-free entry, seated work options, quiet class placement, and advance accommodation conversations.

Open studio and membership

Shared workspace, clear expectations

Open studio is supervised practice time for enrolled students and members. Membership adds recurring access, shelf storage, clay purchasing, tool use, critique circles, and priority workshop registration.

  • Reserve a wheel or handbuilding table before arriving.
  • Use studio clay or approved clay bodies only.
  • Clean wheels, splash pans, tables, tools, and floor spots before leaving.
  • Guests are welcome for tours; making time requires registration.

Teaching philosophy

Patient instruction for a technical, tactile process

Our instructors explain the why behind each studio rule: how clay shrinks, why trapped air matters, when glaze runs, how cleanup protects the plumbing, and how shared space stays welcoming. The goal is not perfection on day one. It is enough understanding to return, practice, and keep making.
