About the lane

Built around browsing, not checkout carts.

Larkspur Lane Books is a small neighborhood shop for used literary fiction, mysteries, sturdy paperbacks, and local favorites that neighbors press into one another’s hands. The shelves change often, the recommendations are handwritten, and the back reading room is set aside for book clubs, quiet afternoons, and low-key author nights.

We are here for the reader who wants a reason to stop in: to confirm hours, bring a tidy stack for trade-in credit, ask after a staff pick, or find the next gathering in the reading room.

Trade-in days

Bring a thoughtful stack. Leave with store credit.

Trade-ins happen every Saturday and on the first Tuesday evening of each month. We review books at the counter while you browse.

  1. Choose up to 10 books. Clean used fiction, mysteries, paperbacks, and recent local-interest titles are easiest for us to place.
  2. Visit during trade-in hours. Saturday 10 AM–2 PM, or first Tuesday 5–7 PM. No appointment needed for small stacks.
  3. Receive shop credit. Credit can be used the same day or saved for a future visit; books we cannot take can go back home with you.

Getting here

We are a short walk from the Elm Street bus stop, with bicycle racks on the lane and two-hour street parking nearby.

Accessibility

The front entrance is step-free. Aisles are kept clear, and staff can pull books or arrange chairs on request.

Group visits

Book clubs and small reading groups can ask about reserving the back table on weeknights.
