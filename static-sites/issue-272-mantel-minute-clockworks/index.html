Repair boundaries

Safe home maintenance is not a full movement rebuild.

We make beginner tasks approachable while clearly marking mainspring, wheel-train, and bushing work that deserves a professional clock repairer.

Comfortable at home

  • Documenting a movement before removing parts
  • Finding a missing key with a gauge
  • Cleaning dust from case interiors
  • Applying a tiny drop of clock oil to mapped pivot points
  • Replacing simple case hardware, pads, or glass retainers

Stop and call a professional

  • Letting down or removing a mainspring without proper training
  • Forcing a key, arbor, hand, or stuck winding train
  • Bushing worn plates or reshaping pivots
  • Deep movement disassembly after a clock has sentimental value
  • Cleaning painted or paper dials with unknown solvents
