About the room

Built for the kind of show where the last note matters.

The room is intentionally small, warmly lit, and organized around listening. Families can understand the plan before arriving. Students can try a first set without feeling exposed. Performers get a calm stage, clear communication, and an audience close enough to hear the quiet parts.

What to know before you come

All-ages by default

Shows are scheduled early when possible, with straightforward age guidance listed on each event card.

Reserved without friction

Send a short note to hold seats. Walk-ins are welcome when there is room; suggested donations are posted clearly.

Listening-room etiquette

We keep the performance space quiet during songs and save conversation for the lobby and sidewalk bench.

Booking

A real room for careful sets.

Send a short introduction, two representative recordings, preferred dates, and whether your set is seated, spoken-word, workshop, or songwriter-focused. We are especially interested in acoustic projects, community showcases, and touring performers who fit an attentive all-ages room.

House basics

  • Simple front-of-house sound support
  • Small stage, seated floor, warm room light
  • Door language agreed before announcement
  • Family-safe show copy and clear start times

Accessibility

Plain information, fewer surprises.

We list door times, seating style, age guidance, and room expectations on every event. Contact us before a show for seating needs, early entry, low-stimulation arrival, or performer access details.
