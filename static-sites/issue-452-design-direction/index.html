How it works

Order at the counter. Ask at the bench.

During the day, the café is a dependable neighborhood habit: coffee, lunch, a quiet corner, and a pin board that stays current. On repair nights, volunteers help diagnose simple fixes while you stay with your item and learn the next step.

Accessibility note

Clear paths, printed guidance, patient help.

Step-free entrance on Brick Lane. Large-print menus and bring-guides are at the till. If the bench is busy, we give you a numbered tag and an honest wait time.

Bring-or-don’t-bring guidance

A good repair night starts with the right item.

Bring
  • One item you can carry by yourself.
  • Chargers, batteries, missing screws, or spare buttons if you have them.
  • A clear description of what changed and when.
  • Patience for a numbered bench queue.
Please don’t bring
  • Microwaves, gas appliances, or anything leaking fuel.
  • Large furniture, built-in fixtures, or heavy appliances.
  • Items you need guaranteed fixed the same night.
  • More than one item per person when the room is full.

Volunteer call
