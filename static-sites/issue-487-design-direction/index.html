From damaged piece to room-ready outcome

A repair sequence that keeps first-time restorers steady.

  1. 01

    Name the problem

    Match the mark, wobble, missing pull, or worn edge to a tray before buying materials.

  2. 02

    Test in a quiet spot

    Every kit includes a small stop-check so the first move happens under the piece, not across the front.

  3. 03

    Work in measured passes

    Guides use grit ranges, drying times, and pressure notes rather than vague encouragement.

  4. 04

    Finish when it is sound

    The goal is a cared-for inherited piece, not a showroom surface stripped of its history.

Bench-room guidance

Not sure which tray fits? Read the marks like a repair note.

Use the quick bench guide to compare common signs: raised grain, milky finish, rubbed corners, dark scratches, loose rails, and screw holes that no longer bite.

Before you begin

  • Photograph the piece from all sides.
  • Mark the underside with tape before testing.
  • Stop if finish transfers heavily to the cloth.
  • Let wax, glue, or filler cure before judging the result.
