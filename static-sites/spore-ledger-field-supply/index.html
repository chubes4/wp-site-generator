Specimen handling

Keep the evidence legible.

Crushed caps, wet bundles, loose berries, and mystery bags make verification harder. Spore Ledger separates the supply wall into breathable transport, rigid protection, labeled containment, and cooler-ready inserts so each find keeps its context.

  • Waxed paper bags for individual mushroom samples
  • Breathable mesh harvest bags for greens and seaweeds
  • Labeled sample envelopes for small or fragile finds
  • Rigid trays for caps, stems, berries, and nuts
Field note

“One sample per pocket” is a system, not a slogan. If you cannot match the specimen to the habitat note later, it stays out of the kitchen.

Identification support

Small tools for disciplined observation.

Measure

Folding rulers and color reference cards make field notes more useful when a club member, instructor, or chef reviews a sample.

Magnify

Hand lenses support checks on gills, pores, leaf edges, stems, berries, and seaweed texture before harvest containers fill up.

Record

Waterproof pencils and notebooks capture location, substrate, habitat, weather, and collection limits in rough conditions.

Tag

Sample tags, quarantine labels, and club-meeting cards help keep discussion tied to the correct specimen.
