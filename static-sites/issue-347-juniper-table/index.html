About Juniper Table

Built for the people who pass the window every week.

Juniper Table started as a morning counter in a narrow room between studios and row houses. The café still works best that way: practical, small, and familiar enough that the barista remembers who needs a quiet corner.

The supper club grew from neighbors asking whether the lunch board ever stayed open after dark. Once a month, the team clears the bulletin board, moves the chairs into one line, and cooks a meal that feels more like being invited in than being seated out.

Small gatherings

A lived-in room for careful, modest occasions.

Juniper Table can host small daytime gatherings, neighborhood meetings, studio breakfasts, and quiet evening meals when the calendar allows.

Private basics

Best for 10–24 people, seated or standing. We keep menus short: coffee and pastry trays, lunch boards, soup-and-salad spreads, or one shared supper menu.

What we need

Tell us the date, headcount, rough budget, timing, and any accessibility or dietary needs. We will let you know quickly whether the room is a fit.

Not a banquet hall

No stage lighting, no bottle-service energy, no huge sound system. The charm is the same as café hours: warm light, close tables, and an easy room.

Room notes

Natural light, market plates, human traces.
