Recurring community rhythms

What happens around the tables.

Low-pressure evenings are part of the room. Drop in, bring a friend, or ask at the counter if you want to host something small and neighborly.

Mondays · 6 PM

Cribbage Table

Open pegs, rotating partners, and no tournament face required. New players can learn a hand before the second coffee goes cold.

Wednesdays · 5 PM

Open Chess

Quiet boards near the windows, clocks optional. A house set lives behind the counter for anyone who forgot theirs.

First Fridays

Tiny Zine Swap

Bring a folded page, a photocopied story, or a stack of local flyers. The noticeboard gets cleared and rebuilt together.

Sundays · 7 PM

Acoustic Listening Night

Small sets, seated room, and a soft close. Local musicians can inquire about a future date below.

About the room

A corner store renovated without sanding off its memory.

Juniper Yard was opened by two counter people who wanted the old corner shop to stay useful to the block. The shelves became game storage, the front windows became morning seats, and the side yard became a tiny patio for mild afternoons.

The point is not to be precious. It is to be steady: lights on early, soup on at lunch, tables that welcome lingering, and events that let neighbors recognize one another.

Find us

118 Juniper & Ash, two blocks from the 12 bus and one block east of the neighborhood library.

Bike racks sit beside the patio gate. Street parking is easiest on Ash after 5 PM.

Accessibility

Step-free front entry, accessible restroom, movable chairs, and a quieter back table available on request when the room is busy.
