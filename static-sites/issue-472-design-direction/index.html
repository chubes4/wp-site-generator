Instructors and neighbors

A workshop that explains the whole process, not just the pretty shelf.

Instructors demonstrate at the wheel, pause at each table, and talk plainly about mistakes: cracked handles, heavy bottoms, surprise glaze runs, rushed drying, and how to make the next piece better.

“The best first pot is the one that teaches your hands what the clay is doing.”

Before you come in

Useful details deserve a place on the page.

Hours

Tuesday–Friday, 12–9pm
Saturday–Sunday, 10am–6pm
Closed Mondays except enrolled classes.

Location & transit

48 Warehouse Lane, beside the canal bridge. Bike racks at the brick wall; two blocks from the Canal stop.

What to wear

Comfortable clothes that can get dusty, trimmed nails if you can, and closed-toe shoes. Aprons are available.

Firing timeline

Most student work is dry, bisqued, glazed, and glaze-fired in three to five weeks. Rush firing is not available.

Accessibility

Step-free entry, adjustable-height tables, wide studio aisles, and quiet corner seating. Email ahead for wheel height needs.

Studio care

Members clean wheels, wipe glaze drips, label every piece, and keep shared shelves orderly for the next maker.

Ready when you are

Start with one class, a question at the door, or a slow walk past the shelves.
