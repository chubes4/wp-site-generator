Repair by use case

A calmer way to pick the right repair.

Start with what failed, then choose the method. Mendstead’s kit structure helps careful hosts, quilters, renters, homesteaders, and linen-keepers buy only what belongs with the piece they are saving.

Field notes

Practical repair knowledge for a working home.

Guide · 6 min read

How to decide whether a repair should be visible or quiet

Choose contrast when the textile already carries history; choose tonal reinforcement when the cloth needs to return to the table without announcement.

Checklist · 4 min read

What to inspect before storing seasonal quilts

Look for batting migration, seam strain, edge abrasion, and the small weak places that become large repairs next winter.

Method · 5 min read

Reinforcing towel hems without making them stiff

Use flexible thread paths, washed patch fabric, and a lighter hand so repaired towels still dry properly and fold cleanly.
