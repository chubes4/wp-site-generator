Before you arrive

A recital hall with the ease of a neighborhood coffee counter.

Shows are seated, conversational before the set, and attentive once music begins. The room is sized for direct sound: close enough to see hands on strings, quiet enough for spoken introductions, and comfortable for mixed-age audiences.

  • Doors usually open 30 minutes before music.
  • Most concerts run 60–90 minutes with one short pause.
  • Families are welcome; we mark extra-gentle shows on the calendar.

Visitor information

Hours

Open for ticketed events and weekend afternoon recitals. Counter service starts at doors and closes after the final set.

Use the municipal lot behind Willow Street after 6 PM. Bike racks sit by the side entrance under the amber sign.

Step-free entry, accessible restroom, armless chairs on request, and reserved front-row space for mobility devices.

Most covers are $10–$18 and posted before you arrive. Student showcases include pay-what-you-can options.

For musicians

Book the room when the room can honor the music.

Lantern Room is best for attentive sets, small ensembles, readings with music, low-volume experimental work, and community recitals. Send a short note with your format, expected draw, stage needs, and two possible dates.

Common questions

Small-room details

Can children attend evening shows?

Yes. All events are all-ages unless a calendar note says otherwise. We simply ask families to step into the lobby if a little listener needs a reset.

Is it okay to talk during sets?

Conversation is welcome before music and during breaks. Once the set starts, the room shifts into respectful listening.

Do I need a ticket in advance?

Advance reservations help us keep seating comfortable, but many weeknight events hold a few door spots.

What is served at the counter?

Coffee, tea, still water, seltzer, and a small tray of pastries. The counter is quiet by design.
