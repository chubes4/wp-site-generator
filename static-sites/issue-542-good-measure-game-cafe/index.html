A café where the rules get explained before the fun has to start.

Walk in for coffee, settle into a two-top, ask for a recommendation, or join a low-pressure event. The room is lively without being loud, social without being forced, and practical about the basics: hours, table policy, food, accessibility, and what to expect on your first visit.

How table time works

Drop in for an hour or make a night of it.

01

Choose your table

Drop-ins are welcome for pairs and small groups. Reservations are encouraged after 6 PM or for parties of five or more.

02

Tell us your mood

Staff guides ask about group size, energy level, time limit, and experience, then pull a short list that fits.

03

Learn without pressure

We teach the first round for many library favorites, mark quiet tables, and keep events friendly to newcomers.

04

Refill and reset

Order from the counter, swap games between rounds, or ask for a shorter pick if the evening changes shape.

Good to know

  • All ages welcome; evening events are best for ages 10+ unless noted.
  • Wheelchair-accessible entrance, restroom, and several adjustable-height tables.
  • Quiet afternoon tables are available weekdays before 4 PM.
  • Private side room fits 8–12 for birthdays, team socials, and small parties.

First visit FAQs

No secret handshake required.

Do I need to know how to play?

No. Staff recommendations and teach-to-play events are built for beginners.

Can I come alone?

Yes. Drop-in nights and community tables are designed to make solo visits feel easy.

Can we bring our own game?

Absolutely. Table time includes the library, but personal games are welcome.

Is there a time limit?

Most tables are flexible. During peak weekend evenings, reservations are held in two-hour blocks.
