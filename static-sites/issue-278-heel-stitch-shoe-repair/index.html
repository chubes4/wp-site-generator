Can it be repaired?

Most worn heels, separated soles, loose stitching, broken straps, bag handles, zippers, and leather scuffs can be evaluated at the counter.

How estimates work

We inspect the item, explain options, note limits, and quote before work begins. Payment is due when the repair is approved.

Typical turnaround

Small fixes often take 2–4 business days. Resoling, stitching, and specialty parts may take 1–2 weeks.

First visit guide

Walk in with worn items. Leave with a clear repair plan.

We built our counter process for people who are not sure whether a repair makes sense. A cobbler will look at wear, material condition, cost, and the life left in the item before recommending work.

What to bring

  • Both shoes or boots, even if only one side is damaged
  • Loose heels, buckles, straps, tags, or hardware if you still have them
  • Clean bags or leather goods emptied of personal items
  • Photos ahead of time for costume pieces, large drop-offs, or mobility needs

When we may advise against repair

Some cracked synthetics, dry-rotted soles, waterlogged interiors, and weakened leather cannot hold a durable stitch or adhesive. We will say so plainly before you spend money.

Seasonal care

Small maintenance keeps favorite items in circulation.

Care advice changes with the weather. Ask at the counter for the right brush, conditioner, polish, or waterproofing approach before treating suede, smooth leather, patent finishes, or synthetic blends.

Winter

Boot waterproofing & salt stains

Bring boots before the first storm for protective treatment, or after slush for salt lifting and conditioning.

Spring

Suede brushing & sandal prep

Refresh nap, replace worn buckles, and repair straps before warm weather turns small splits into failures.

Fall

Back-to-school checks

We look for loose soles, worn heels, torn backpack straps, and quick fixes that stretch the year’s budget.

A useful item deserves a knowledgeable look before it gets replaced.
