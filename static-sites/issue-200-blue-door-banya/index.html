First-visit guide

Before you walk through the blue door.

If you are unsure about etiquette, pacing, accessibility, modesty, or whether heat and cold are right for you, ask before booking or when you arrive. Staff can help you choose a calmer hour, skip the plunge, or plan extra rest.

  1. Arrive ten minutes early

    Check in, receive towels, store your things, and hear the house rhythm before entering shared spaces.

  2. Warm slowly

    Start with short heat rounds. Sit lower if you want less intensity. Hydrate between rounds.

  3. Use cold by choice

    Cold plunge windows are staff-timed and optional. A cool shower is always an acceptable reset.

  4. Rest like it counts

    Bench time is part of the session. Keep voices low during quiet hours and make room for neighbors.

House etiquette

Shared space works when everyone knows the basics.

What to wear

Wear a swimsuit or comfortable bathwear in mixed areas. Staff can explain modesty options and quieter changing times.

Phones stay tucked away

No photography in changing, sauna, steam, plunge, or lounge areas. The bathhouse should feel private and easy.

Health considerations

Heat and cold are intense. Check with a clinician if pregnant, managing heart or blood-pressure concerns, or unsure about cold exposure.

Age policy

Guests 16–17 are welcome with an adult during open sessions. Community nights may post different age guidance.
