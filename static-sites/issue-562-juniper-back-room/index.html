What kind of place is this?

A calm alternative to the café playlist and the late-night venue.

Enter through the side hall behind Juniper Café and the block gets softer: shelves of staff notes, a small bar, a careful turntable, and just enough seats for everyone to feel close to the record. We host album evenings, songwriter sets, folk and jazz duos, and private gatherings that respect the listening room.

Small-format shows

Close enough to hear the wood in the instrument.

We book low-volume music that fits a room of listeners: solo songwriters, folk duos, jazz trios without amplification creep, visiting poets with a guitar case, and rare afternoon sessions when the café is still open up front.

  • Most shows are seated and capped at 32 guests.
  • Sets begin on time so neighbors can plan a calm night out.
  • Performers get clear load-in notes and a respectful room.

House rules

Respectful listening keeps the room generous.

01

Phones dimmed

Photos are fine before and after sets. During album sides, keep screens low and quiet.

02

Conversation below the music

There is room to talk, but the record or performer should never have to compete.

03

Ask about access needs

The room is compact. Contact us before your visit and we will help with seating, timing, and entry details.

Booking & private gatherings

Bring music that fits close walls.

Musicians, hosts, and neighbors planning a small gathering can write with a short note about the sound, date range, and room needs. We are best for listening sessions, acoustic sets, readings, low-key celebrations, and groups that want a bar without a roar.
