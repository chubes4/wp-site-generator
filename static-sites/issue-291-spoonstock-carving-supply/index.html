Buying guides

Start with the question at your bench.

Guide blocks compare tool shapes, blanks, and maintenance steps in the same plain language used on the product cards.

01

Which knife first?

Use a sloyd knife for outside shaping, a hook knife for bowls and spoon hollows, and a detail knife only after the form is established.

02

Dry blank or green billet?

Dry blanks keep apartment projects predictable. Green billets carve beautifully but need drying space, labels, and slower expectations.

03

What sharpening setup?

Pair a medium water stone with a slip stone and leather strop. Keep the bevel visible and measure progress before chasing polish.

04

Where do hands go?

Set the knife down between decisions, carve away from fingers, and use guards before fatigue makes the cut for you.
